Sunken Rectangles

2017, Ongoing

The Black cemeteries of Richmond, Virginia represent the struggles, oppression, dignity, and aspirations of over 300 years of Richmond’s Black communities. However, each of these cemeteries is in or has faced varied states of abandonment and disrepair, due to both direct racial prejudice and the indirect effects of that prejudice. These photographs approach the cemeteries as historical sites, attempting to record the visible history of their past. But the photographs also serve as records of the present, of the continuing systematic injustice acting on the cemeteries and the work being done to counter its effects. Ultimately, I hope to implicate the development of Richmond in the historic and ongoing injustices that I see revealed in the city’s Black cemeteries.
