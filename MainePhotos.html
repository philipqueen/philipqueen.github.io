Rockland Maine

March 2017

The following are images of scenes in and around Rockland, Maine during an uncharacteristic warm spell in March, 2017. Rockland was an industrial center in the 19th century, specializing in shipbuilding, and while its economy is increasingly driven by tourism and regional services, it retains a diverse industrial sector. Rockland hosts the Maine Lobster Festival annually, which claims the "Worlds Largest Lobster Cooker" and the subject of David Foster Wallace's 2004 essay "Consider the Lobster." Whether due to its manufacturing or its fishing, Rockland is contrasted to the neighboring town of Camden with the saying "Camden by the sea, Rockland by the smell." As the home of the Farnsworth Art Museum and the Center for Maine Contemporary Art along with numerous galleries, and with a population of around 7,000 people, Rockland has an exceptionally high amount of art per capita.
